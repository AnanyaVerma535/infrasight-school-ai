About InfraSight

InfraSight focuses on identifying poor and unsafe school infrastructure, especially where development funds may have been released but conditions remain unacceptable.

Why this platform exists

Many government schools still face cracked walls, leaking roofs, damaged toilets, unsafe wiring and unusable classrooms. InfraSight gives citizens a simple way to report these issues and creates a visible record that officers can review.

Instead of relying only on paperwork, the platform collects direct visual evidence of bad infrastructure. This helps strengthen transparency, accountability and timely intervention in public education projects.

How reporting works now

Citizens upload one clear image of the bad condition, add location and school details, and describe the issue. Officers then review the complaint and mark it as pending, verified or flagged depending on inspection findings and fund-use concerns.

How AI can support verification

In the next stage, AI can help classify visible damage patterns such as roof leakage, wall cracks, broken furniture zones and unsafe facilities. It can also help prioritize severe reports and identify schools where reported repairs do not match current conditions on the ground.
